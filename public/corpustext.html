Welcome to VOICE 3.0 Online BETA!

You are currently accessing VOICE 3.0 Online BETA. This is a pre-release version of the new web interface of the Vienna-Oxford International Corpus of English, a one-million-word, computer-readable corpus of spoken ELF interactions. The VOICE 3.0 Online interface is being developed in the project VOICE CLARIAH (2020-2021) with funding from CLARIAH-AT by an interdisciplinary team at the Austrian Center for Digital Humanities and Cultural Heritage (ACDH-CH) at the Austrian Academy of Sciences (Ã–AW) and the English Department of the University of Vienna.

VOICE 3.0 Online BETA will be available for testing for a limited period of time only (about 10 days) at the end of April 2021. Please provide us with feedback on your user experience by filling out our survey. Your comments will substantially support the final development of the new VOICE user interface.

The official release of VOICE 3.0 Online is planned for summer 2021.

Explore the new features of VOICE 3.0 Online BETA

Enhanced search functions

In VOICE 3.0 Online BETA, the previous interfaces VOICE Online and VOICE POS Online have been merged and refined. In addition, our new interface includes entirely new query options that make it possible to search for select mark-up of spoken language (pauses, laughter, overlaps, etc.). Queries for tokens, POS, lemmas and mark-up can be flexibly combined and modified with different wildcards. For details of query features to try out, see our VOICE 3.0 Online BETA search manual.

Additional filter categories

In addition to domain and speech event type, VOICE 3.0 Online BETA offers new filter categories for selecting speech events (number of interactants/speakers, duration, number of words, etc.) and building your own subcorpus.

New style options

Try out the enhanced, customizable display of search results and VOICE transcripts. Have a look at our style options (voice, plain, pos, xml; kwic style for search results) and/or adapt the voice style according to your research interests by using the different 'flexi style' categories (overlaps, pauses, etc.; new: e.g. laughter, uncertain transcription).
